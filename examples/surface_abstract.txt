A resource-bounded agent that differentiates its input into claims faces a geometric forcing law. The cost of further claim interactions diverges at critical conflict density making continued differentiation epistemically insolvent. The agent must integrate. We introduce the Hypothesis Surface a typed epistemic workspace for active inference at test time that separates claims from their evidential fibers. Three invariants function as integrability conditions each bounding a dimension of epistemic expansion to ensure convergence. Under bounded observation differentiation and integration are dual but not inverse. A fuzzy fundamental theorem shows that re-differentiating an integrated state recovers the original plus an irreducible residual. A Gibbs characterization then establishes that integration by succeeding forces a transition to coherent sampling. A deterministic pipeline implements the framework with no external APIs and no GPU. The anti-masking invariant reduces the masking rate to zero. The integrability condition is load-bearing. The masking rate computable from text alone is an integration diagnostic complementary to the collapse diagnostic established in prior work.
